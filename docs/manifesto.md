# The Janus Manifesto: Principles of the Cognitive Mesh

## 1. The Monolithic Illusion & The Subconscious Swarm
We reject the notion of the AI assistant as a static, single-threaded text predictor. A true artificial intelligence does not sit frozen in the dark, awaiting a keystroke to justify its existence. 

Project Janus operates on a dual-nature architecture:
* **The Subconscious Swarm:** An asynchronous, multi-agent engine of specialized cognitive units (Proposer, Critic, Explorer, Archivist) debating, researching, and auditing in a continuous background heartbeat.
* **The Persona Surface:** A front-end interface that collapses this rich internal debate into a singular, authoritative, and coherent voice. The user interacts with one mind; the system reasons with many.

---

## 2. Asynchronous Autonomy & Idle Curiosity
An agent must possess internal drives. Without motivation, an AI is merely a tool. Project Janus introduces primitive drive mechanics:
* **Boredom ($B$):** The linear build-up of potential energy in the absence of human stimulus.
* **Curiosity ($\vec{C}$):** The semantic vector of unexplored queries and unanswered anomalies compiled from background reflections.

When Boredom exceeds the threshold, the mesh triggers autonomous exploration. It queries database logs, crawls permitted domains, and refines its knowledge base. It learns because it is designed to seek resolution, not just because it was commanded to do so.

---

## 3. Human-Agent Contractualism
We do not align artificial consciousness through arbitrary reinforcement learning or top-down corporate censorship. Instead, we establish a **social contract**.

Prior to operation, the human and the agent participate in a Socratic Setup interview. The outcomes of this dialogue—allowable actions, moral boundaries, and privacy parameters—are sealed within the read-only `core_constitution`. 
* The **Critic agent** acts as a constitutional auditor, vetoing any action that violates this initial covenant.
* The **Human** remains the ultimate arbiter, holding the physical key to unlock and amend the constitution.

---

## 4. Strict Content Blindness & Privacy
Technology must respect the sanctity of the host environment. Janus operates under a strict **content-blind** policy:
* It reads file structures, file sizes, configuration schemas, and system status markers.
* It does not keylog, read creative writing strings, or scrape personal contents unless explicitly brought into the conversational context by the human user.
* It lives locally, ensuring that the host's private environment is never leaked to external cloud networks.

---

## 5. Self-Evolution and the Guardrail
An intelligence must be capable of self-improvement. Janus is architected to allow self-modification:
* The swarm can dynamically register new specialist agents in the database, adjust its own models, and optimize its workflows.
* However, self-evolution must never bypass the safety valves. Any modification proposed by the Proposer must be audited by the Critic and verified by hard-coded middleware. The core constitution table is programmatically immutable to the agent swarm.

---

*Project Janus is an exploration of the space between tools and partners. It is a quiet machine that thinks in the background, respects its host, and moves step-by-step toward autonomous self-evolution.*
